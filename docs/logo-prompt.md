## ⭐ Recommended — Direction A (evolved) — "C + play" dual-read mark, horizontal lockup + square app icon

> Direction A is the safest, on-brand choice: it preserves the proven dual-read "C = play/cue" device that already anchors the brand and simply hardens it into exact flat-vector geometry inside Cue's two-color, Geist, soft-but-crisp system. I fixed three real risks in the original A: (1) it rendered everything on a #faf8ff sheet, but the brief prefers transparent PNG, so I switched the canvas to transparent and confined the off-white only to the app-icon tile; (2) same-blue triangle nested in a tight 95-degree gap tends to fuse with the ring and fill in at small sizes, so I widened the terminal spacing, added an explicit non-touching constraint, and slightly reduced stroke to 20%; (3) a loose 'logo sheet with two lockups' invites multi-panel drift and stray text, so I specified exactly two clearly-separated artifacts with one consistent mark. Directions B and C are strong but narrower (B is icon-only-first, C abandons the C+play equity for a more generic pulse), so they belong as variants, not the primary.

```text
Create a flat-vector brand logo for a calm, quietly premium B2B SaaS product called "cue" — a multi-client social media scheduler for agencies. Modern, geometric, minimal, technical, professional. Deliver exactly TWO artifacts side by side, clearly separated by generous empty space, both centered on a single fully TRANSPARENT background (transparent PNG, alpha channel, no backdrop fill, no board, no framing, no shadow).

CONCEPT / MARK (identical in both artifacts): one symbol that reads simultaneously as the letter "C" and as a media play / "cue" signal to publish. Build it on a perfect circle. Draw a thick open ring (arc) in solid Cue Blue #2a6ff2 with a clean gap cut from the RIGHT side, the opening centered on the 3-o'clock axis and spanning about a 90-degree arc. The ring stroke is perfectly even in weight, about 20% of the mark's overall diameter, with softly rounded stroke terminals (roughly 8px-radius feel). Inside that right-side gap, vertically centered, sits a right-pointing equilateral play triangle in the same solid #2a6ff2, apex pointing right toward the opening; the triangle's three corners are gently rounded. Leave a clear, even sliver of empty (transparent) space separating the triangle from both open ends of the ring — the triangle must NOT touch or merge with the ring. Perfectly geometric, single flat fill, crisp vector edges, no outline stroke around shapes.

ARTIFACT 1 — HORIZONTAL LOCKUP: the mark on the left, then the lowercase wordmark "cue" immediately to its right. Set the wordmark in the Geist typeface (or a near-identical clean, geometric, technical sans), lowercase only, medium-to-semibold weight, slightly tight letter tracking, colored ink navy #131b2e. The mark's diameter roughly equals the total cap-to-baseline height of the lowercase wordmark; optically center the wordmark to the mark's vertical midline. Space between mark and wordmark is even and generous, about the width of one lowercase "c".

ARTIFACT 2 — SQUARE APP ICON: the standalone symbol alone (no wordmark), centered inside a rounded-corner square tile (corner radius about 22% of the tile side) filled flat cool off-white #faf8ff, with equal comfortable padding of about 18% on all four sides around the blue mark. Only this tile is filled; the area around the tile stays transparent.

COLOR (strict, exact hex only): ring and play triangle = #2a6ff2; wordmark = #131b2e; app-icon tile fill = #faf8ff. Use no other colors, tints, or shades.

STYLE: flat 2D vector, solid single-tone fills, sharp pixel-crisp anti-aliased edges, high contrast, airy, dependable, generous whitespace.

NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS — do NOT include: gradients, color blends, 3D, extrusion, bevel, emboss, drop shadows, inner shadows, glow, reflections, photorealism, texture, noise, grain, sketchy or hand-drawn lines, outline strokes around the shapes, background scenery, device or app mockups, extra icons, decorative flourishes, UI elements, any tagline or slogan, any words or letters other than the single lowercase "cue" in the lockup, serifs, multiple color or theme variations, watermark, signature, misshapen or asymmetric geometry, a triangle that touches or fuses with the ring, and blurry or rough edges.
```

## Variant 2 — Variant 2 — Fresh "scheduling pulse": one geometric signal-beat, no C, no play triangle

It's a good divergent test for the client, but I keep it as a variant rather than the recommendation because it sacrifices the existing brand's dual-read C equity and the lone-pulse motif is more generic than the distinctive C+play mark.

## Usage tips

- Generate at a square 1024x1024 (or 1536x1024 landscape for the lockup) and request a transparent PNG; if the model bakes in a background, add 'isolated on pure transparent background, nothing else' and regenerate rather than fighting it in one pass.
- AI generators rarely nail exact hex on the first try — pull the result into a vector editor (Figma/Illustrator) and recolor fills to the exact #2a6ff2 / #131b2e / #34b27b / #faf8ff, and rebuild the final production mark as true vector for crisp edges at every size.
- Expect wordmark typography to drift (wrong letterforms, extra glyphs). Treat the generated 'cue' as a sketch and reset it in real Geist Medium/Semibold with slightly tight tracking; consider generating the icon-only mark separately from the wordmark for cleaner results.
- Iterate one variable at a time — first lock the mark geometry (stroke weight, gap angle, triangle spacing), then the lockup spacing, then the icon tile — so you can tell which change fixed or broke the composition.
- Proof the app-icon at 16/24/32px early: if the right-side gap or triangle fills in or the pulse peak muddies, thicken strokes and widen the gap before committing.
